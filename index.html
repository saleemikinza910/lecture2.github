My Story

Kinza Saleemi — software engineering student & creative explorer bridging code, culture, and curiosity

Kinza Saleemi — currently pursuing software engineering, I see every line of code as a new sentence in a larger narrative. My journey fuses logical thinking with creative wanderlust: from debugging algorithms to navigating foreign streets, from reading literary classics to binge‑watching thought‑provoking series. I believe that the most innovative engineers are also storytellers, language learners, and travelers at heart. This is my evolving story.

✦ Where logic meets curiosity

I grew up surrounded by both computers and colourful travel diaries. At sixteen, I wrote my first Python script and fell in love with the elegance of problem-solving. But I also loved discovering new cultures — flipping through language guides, planning imaginary itineraries. Today, as a software engineering student, I'm channeling that same energy into building tools that are functional, inclusive, and human‑centered. My room is a hybrid space: whiteboard full of system designs on one wall, a world map dotted with pins on the other.

🌍 Traveling, languages & the art of immersion

For me, traveling isn't just about sightseeing — it's a lesson in adaptability. From the chaotic charm of Istanbul's bazaars to the quiet mornings in the Scottish Highlands, I collect stories, not souvenirs. This passion naturally grew into a love for languages: I'm fluent in Urdu and English, currently learning Japanese (to read untranslated Murakami and survive spontaneous Tokyo trips), and dabbling in Spanish. Each new language unlocks a different way to think — both in conversation and in code.

📚 Reading, series & narrative obsession

Between semesters, I disappear into books — I love the emotional depth of authors like Ocean Vuong and the clarity of technical memoirs. My favorite series? Anything that plays with nonlinear storytelling and character complexity: Arcane for its stunning craft, The Bear for its raw energy, and Dark for its mind‑bending structure. I believe great series, like great software, rely on consistent internal logic and meaningful callbacks. They inspire how I design user experiences: layered, rewarding, and empathetic.
